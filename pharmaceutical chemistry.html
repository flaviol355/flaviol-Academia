Pharmaceutical Chemistry

Here are some questions and answers to help you understand pharmaceutical chemistry better.

Questions and Answers

  1. What is autoxidation? Explain the mechanism of drug autoxidation. (5 marks)
    • Answer: Autoxidation is a chemical reaction in which a substance reacts with oxygen in the air, leading to oxidation. The mechanism involves:
      • Initiation: Formation of free radicals due to external factors like light or heat.
      • Propagation: Free radicals react with oxygen to produce peroxides, leading to further radical formation.
      • Termination: Radical reactions end when stable compounds are formed, halting the autoxidation process.
  2. Explain factors that affect the autoxidation process. (5 marks)
    • Answer: Factors include:
      • Presence of light, which can accelerate radical formation.
      • Temperature, as higher temperatures increase reaction rates.
      • Metal ions, which act as catalysts.
      • Oxygen concentration, affecting the extent of oxidation.
      • Drug formulation, with some additives acting as antioxidants.
  3. How can drug oxidation during storage be minimized? (5 marks)
    • Answer: Oxidation can be minimized by:
      • Reducing oxygen exposure through airtight packaging.
      • Using antioxidants in the formulation.
      • Storing in dark containers to prevent light exposure.
      • Controlling storage temperature.
      • Adding metal chelating agents to reduce catalytic effects of metals.
  4. Explain isomerization as a means of drug degradation. (5 marks)
    • Answer: Isomerization is the rearrangement of molecules within a drug, changing its structure without altering its molecular formula. This can affect drug efficacy and stability, often influenced by factors like pH, light, and temperature.
  5. When antiseptic formaldehyde solution is stored in the cold, a white precipitate appears. Explain why. (3 marks)
    • Answer: At low temperatures, formaldehyde polymerizes to form paraformaldehyde, which appears as a white precipitate.
  6. Using a chemical equation, show how procaine undergoes hydrolysis and then decarboxylation. (3 marks)
    • Answer:
      • Hydrolysis: Procaine + H2O → para-aminobenzoic acid + ethanol
      • Decarboxylation: para-aminobenzoic acid → aniline + CO2
  7. Explain physical degradation of drugs with examples. (10 marks)
    • Answer: Physical degradation includes changes in the physical state of a drug, such as:
      • Polymorphism: Different crystalline forms, e.g., steroids.
      • Hygroscopicity: Absorption of moisture, e.g., sodium chloride.
      • Efflorescence: Loss of water from a hydrated compound, e.g., citric acid.
  8. Differentiate between sedatives and hypnotics. (2 marks)
    • Answer: Sedatives are drugs that calm the central nervous system, reducing anxiety, while hypnotics are drugs that induce sleep, often used for treating insomnia.
  9. Discuss the SAR of sedatives-hypnotic drugs. (10 marks)
    • Answer: Structure-Activity Relationship (SAR) of sedative-hypnotic drugs includes:
      • The presence of halogens and alkyl groups, enhancing CNS depressant activity.
      • Specific molecular arrangements that influence the duration and intensity of sedative effects.
