ICT Questions and Answers

Below are some key questions and answers in ICT to help you with your studies.

Questions and Answers

  1. Define the following terms as used in ICT:
    • ICT: Information and Communication Technology, which refers to technologies that provide access to information through telecommunications.
    • Data: Raw, unprocessed facts and figures that can be processed by a computer.
    • Information: Processed data that is meaningful and useful for decision-making.
    • Peripheral devices: External devices connected to a computer, such as a mouse, keyboard, or printer.
  2. Distinguish between analogue and digital computers: Analogue computers process data represented by continuous physical quantities, while digital computers process data in binary form (0s and 1s).
  3. Outline three characteristics of third generation computers:
    • Use of integrated circuits, which improved speed and efficiency.
    • Smaller in size compared to previous generations, making them more portable.
    • Increased reliability and lower power consumption.
  4. Differentiate between RAM and ROM:
    • RAM (Random Access Memory): Temporary memory used by the computer to store data that is currently being processed; data is lost when power is turned off.
    • ROM (Read-Only Memory): Permanent memory used to store critical system information, which remains even when the computer is turned off.
  5. State four disadvantages of computers:
    • Can lead to job displacement as manual tasks become automated.
    • Dependency on electricity and can be expensive to maintain.
    • Risk of cyber-attacks and data breaches.
    • Health issues such as eye strain and back problems due to prolonged use.
  6. Explain two advantages of a desktop PC over a laptop:
    • Desktop PCs usually have better performance and upgrade potential compared to laptops at a similar price point.
    • Desktop PCs are generally more durable for long-term use and easier to repair.
  7. Distinguish between system software and application software, giving an example in each case:
    • System software: Software that manages hardware and system operations, e.g., Operating System (OS).
    • Application software: Software designed to perform specific tasks for the user, e.g., Microsoft Word for document creation.
  8. Give five advantages of a computerized system over a manual system:
    • Faster data processing and retrieval.
    • Improved accuracy and reliability in data handling.
    • Better storage and organization of data.
    • Enhanced security features to protect data.
    • Reduces redundancy and minimizes repetitive tasks.
  9. Give two examples of secondary storage devices:
    • Hard disk drives (HDD).
    • USB flash drives.
  10. Explain one similarity and one difference between human beings and computer systems:
    • Similarity: Both can process information and store data.
    • Difference: Humans have consciousness and emotions, while computers operate on programmed logic and do not have self-awareness.
