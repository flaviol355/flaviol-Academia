Questions and Answers

  1. Define the following terms:
    • Management: The process of coordinating and overseeing the work activities of others so that organizational goals can be accomplished efficiently and effectively.
    • Redundancy: A situation where an employee's position is no longer needed, often leading to job termination due to organizational restructuring or downsizing.
  2. Outline four characteristics of management principles.
    • Universal: Applicable to all types of organizations.
    • Flexible: Can be adapted to different situations.
    • General Guidelines: Provide basic directions rather than strict instructions.
    • Formed by Experimentation: Developed through observation and analysis over time.
  3. Explain why management is referred to as an art.
    • Management requires creativity, intuition, and insight to handle various situations effectively.
    • It involves applying knowledge and skills practically to achieve organizational goals.
    • Managers often have to think on their feet and make unique decisions in different scenarios.
    • Like art, management style can vary depending on the manager’s personality and the organizational context.
  4. State four functions of middle-level management.
    • Implementing organizational plans formulated by top management.
    • Coordinating activities within departments.
    • Providing feedback and insights to top management.
    • Motivating and supporting employees to meet organizational objectives.
  5. List four advantages of job evaluation.
    • Ensures fair compensation by aligning pay with job responsibilities.
    • Helps in creating a systematic pay structure.
    • Facilitates recruitment and retention of skilled employees.
    • Improves employee satisfaction by providing clear career paths.
  6. State four features of manpower planning.
    • Forecasting future manpower needs.
    • Analyzing current manpower resources.
    • Identifying skill gaps and training needs.
    • Developing action plans to fulfill future requirements.
  7. Outline four requirements of a good control system.
    • Accuracy: Should provide reliable information.
    • Timeliness: Information should be available when needed.
    • Flexibility: Adaptable to changing conditions.
    • Cost-effectiveness: Benefits should justify the cost of the system.
  8. Explain two methods of solving conflict in management.
    • Compromise: Both parties give up something to reach a mutually acceptable solution.
    • Collaboration: Working together to find a win-win solution that satisfies both parties' interests.
  9. Elaborate on interpersonal skills of a manager.
    • Figurehead: Carries out ceremonial duties and represents the organization.
    • Leader: Motivates and guides employees towards achieving goals.
    • Liaison: Builds and maintains relationships within and outside the organization.
  10. Describe the Behavioral Science Theory.
    • Focuses on understanding human behavior in the workplace to improve management practices and enhance employee motivation and productivity.
    • Highlights the importance of interpersonal skills, communication, and group dynamics in management.
  11. Describe three management styles.
    • Autocratic: Decisions are made unilaterally by the manager without much input from employees.
    • Democratic: Involves employees in decision-making, fostering collaboration.
    • Laissez-faire: Minimal managerial interference, giving employees autonomy over their work.
  12. Explain the hierarchy of needs as outlined by Abraham Maslow.
    • Physiological Needs: Basic needs such as food, water, shelter, and clothing.
    • Safety Needs: The need for a secure environment and job security.
    • Social Needs: Need for belonging, love, and social interactions.
    • Esteem Needs: Need for respect, recognition, and self-worth.
    • Self-Actualization Needs: Realization of one's potential and personal growth.
  13. Regarding staff training and development:
    • a) Describe two types of training.
      • On-the-Job Training: This type of training is conducted within the workplace while the employee is performing their job. It includes methods like job rotation, coaching, and mentorship, allowing employees to learn by doing.
      • Off-the-Job Training: Training that occurs outside of the regular work environment. This includes seminars, workshops, and courses, which help employees gain new skills and knowledge that they can apply when they return to work.
    • b) Explain five benefits of training to an individual.
      • Skill Improvement: Training helps individuals improve their job-related skills, making them more effective in their roles.
      • Career Advancement: Acquiring new skills through training can enhance an individual's chances of promotion and career growth.
      • Increased Confidence: Training can boost an individual's confidence by making them feel more competent in their job.
      • Adaptability: Training prepares individuals to adapt to new technologies and processes within the organization.
      • Job Satisfaction: Training can increase job satisfaction by enabling employees to perform better and achieve their career goals.
