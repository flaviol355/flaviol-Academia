Pharmacy Practice

Welcome to the Pharmacy Practice page! Here you’ll find notes and questions to help you master this area.

Exam Questions - Section A

  1. Compare and contrast the apothecary and the metric systems. (5 marks)
    The apothecary system is an old system of measurement primarily used in pharmacy and medicine, which includes units like grains, drams, and ounces. The metric system is a modern, decimal-based system used internationally, with units like grams, liters, and meters. Unlike the apothecary system, the metric system is more standardized and easier to convert between units.
  2. Prepare 500 ml Lysol 1:100 for general purpose disinfection. Label appropriately for use in ward 9, Konza hospital. Box, 202, Nairobi. (5 marks)
    To prepare 500 ml of a 1:100 Lysol solution:
    - **Step 1:** Identify the ratio, 1:100, which means 1 part Lysol to 100 parts water.
    - **Step 2:** Calculate the amount of Lysol needed. For 500 ml, divide by 100:
        \( \text{500 ml} \div 100 = 5 \text{ ml Lysol} \)
    - **Step 3:** Add 495 ml of water to make up a final volume of 500 ml.
    - **Final Answer:** 5 ml Lysol + 495 ml water. Label as *500 ml Lysol 1:100 Solution, Ward 9, Konza Hospital, Box 202, Nairobi*.
  3. Define extemporaneous preparations and give 4 reasons for compounding. (5 marks)
    Extemporaneous preparations are medications prepared on-demand to meet specific patient needs. Reasons for compounding include:
    - Customizing dosage forms for patients who cannot use standard forms.
    - Combining multiple medications into a single dose.
    - Eliminating allergens or preservatives.
    - Adding flavors to improve compliance, especially for children.
  4. Outline the procedure for preparation of 25% w/v benzyl benzoate. (5 marks)
    To prepare a 25% w/v benzyl benzoate solution:
    - **Step 1:** Measure 25 grams of benzyl benzoate.
    - **Step 2:** Dissolve it in water and bring the volume to 100 ml.
    - **Final Answer:** This yields a 25% w/v solution. Label with concentration and usage instructions.
  5. If 1 gallon = 160 fluid ounces and 1 ounce = 30 ml, how many gallons are in 10 liters? (5 marks)
    To calculate the gallons in 10 liters:
    - **Convert liters to milliliters:** \( 10 \text{ L} \times 1000 = 10000 \text{ ml} \)
    - **Convert milliliters to ounces:** \( 10000 \text{ ml} \div 30 \text{ ml/oz} = 333.33 \text{ oz} \)
    - **Convert ounces to gallons:** \( 333.33 \text{ oz} \div 160 \text{ oz/gal} = 2.08 \text{ gallons} \)
    - **Final Answer:** 10 liters is approximately 2.08 gallons.
  6. In intravenous therapy, how many points are in 800 ml if 1 point = 20 fluid ounces? (5 marks)
    To determine the points in 800 ml:
    - **Convert ml to ounces:** \( 800 \text{ ml} \div 30 \text{ ml/oz} = 26.67 \text{ oz} \)
    - **Calculate points:** \( 26.67 \text{ oz} \div 20 \text{ oz/point} = 1.33 \text{ points} \)
    - **Final Answer:** There are approximately 1.33 points in 800 ml.
  7. Describe the following devices: (5 marks)
    - **Chemical:** Equipment or tools used for handling and measuring chemicals accurately.
    - **Calibrated dropper:** A dropper marked with graduations for precise liquid measurements.
    - **Pre-filled syringes:** Syringes pre-loaded with specific doses of medication for convenience.
    - **Safety syringes:** Syringes designed to prevent needle-stick injuries.
    - **Weighing balance:** Device used to measure the weight of substances accurately.
  8. Prepare 20 g of ointment using the following formula: (5 marks)
    Formula:
    - **Coal tar** - 4 g
    - **Salicylic acid** - 1 g
    - **Base** - 15 g
    **Steps:**
    1. Weigh 4 g of coal tar and 1 g of salicylic acid.
    2. Mix them together.
    3. Gradually add the base (15 g) while stirring to obtain a homogeneous mixture.
    - **Final Answer:** Label the ointment with ingredients and usage instructions.
  • What are the differences between ointments, creams, and lotions? (5 marks)
    Ointments, creams, and lotions differ in terms of their consistency and intended use:
    - **Ointments:** Thick and greasy, primarily used for dry skin conditions, providing a protective layer.
    - **Creams:** Semi-solid emulsions, less greasy, suitable for moisturizing and treating inflamed skin.
    - **Lotions:** Light, water-based, and less viscous, suitable for covering larger areas, especially for oily skin.
  • Explain the term "bioavailability" and why it is important in pharmacology. (5 marks)
    Bioavailability refers to the proportion of a drug that enters the bloodstream and reaches the site of action. It is important because it determines the correct dosage needed to achieve therapeutic effects without causing toxicity.
  • Describe the process of tablet compression and its significance in tablet manufacturing. (5 marks)
    Tablet compression is the process of compacting powder into a solid dose form using a tablet press. This is significant because it ensures uniformity, stability, and ease of administration, making the medication convenient for patients.
  • List the factors that influence drug absorption in the gastrointestinal tract. (5 marks)
    Factors influencing drug absorption in the gastrointestinal tract include:
    - **pH of the stomach and intestines**
    - **Presence of food**
    - **Drug solubility**
    - **Gastrointestinal motility**
    - **First-pass metabolism** in the liver.
  • Define "half-life" in pharmacokinetics and explain its clinical significance. (5 marks)
    Half-life is the time taken for the plasma concentration of a drug to reduce by half. Clinically, it is important because it determines the dosing frequency to maintain therapeutic levels and avoid toxicity.
  • Dexamethasone solution for injection is in 10ml vial, with concentration of 3mg/ml. If the whole vial is to be diluted 2 fold using normal saline,
    1. Identify the final volume. (2 marks)
      The final volume is 20 ml (10 ml original solution + 10 ml normal saline).
    2. Calculate the quantity of normal saline used. (1 mark)
      Quantity of normal saline used = 10 ml.
    3. Calculate the final concentration. (2 marks)
      Initial concentration = 3 mg/ml
      Since it is diluted 2-fold, the concentration is halved.
      Final concentration = 3 mg/ml ÷ 2 = 1.5 mg/ml.
  • Prepare 500 ml Lysol 1:200 for general purpose disinfection.
    1. Calculate how much of stock you would use. (3 marks)
      To make a 1:200 solution, 1 part of Lysol is mixed with 200 parts of water.
      For 500 ml total solution:
      Volume of Lysol stock = 500 ml / 200 = 2.5 ml.
    2. Calculate much diluent you would use. (2 marks)
      Diluent volume = 500 ml - 2.5 ml = 497.5 ml.
    3. If the initial concentration of Lysol is 40% v/v, calculate the concentration of the final product. (5 marks)
      Initial concentration = 40% v/v
      Dilution factor = 200 (from 1:200 dilution)
      Final concentration = 40% / 200 = 0.2% v/v.
  • 1 liter Oral morphine solution 10% v/v is to be diluted to 2.5% v/v.
    1. Calculate the final volume. (3 marks)
      Using the dilution formula: C1V1 = C2V2
      C1 = 10%, V1 = 1 liter, C2 = 2.5%
      V2 = (C1 × V1) / C2 = (10% × 1 L) / 2.5% = 4 liters.
    2. Find out the fold dilution. (2 marks)
      Fold dilution = Final Volume / Initial Volume = 4 L / 1 L = 4-fold dilution.
  • If the impurities of industrial Mercury in a cough syrup should be less than 0.0005 PPM, express this as a percentage concentration. (5 marks)
    0.0005 PPM (parts per million) means 0.0005 mg of Mercury per liter of solution.
    Converting PPM to percentage:
    0.0005 PPM = 0.0005 mg/L = 0.00000005% (or 5 × 10-8 %).
