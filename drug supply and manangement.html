Questions

  • 1. Discuss the elements of primary healthcare. (5 marks)
    • Health education
    • Adequate nutrition
    • Clean water and sanitation
    • Maternal and child healthcare
    • Immunization
    • Prevention and control of endemic diseases
  • 2. List the principles of primary healthcare. (5 marks)
    • Equity
    • Accessibility
    • Participation
    • Inter-sectoral coordination
    • Sustainability
  • 3. What is the role of community-based distribution programs in primary healthcare? (5 marks)
    • Improve accessibility to healthcare services
    • Provide health education
    • Distribute essential drugs
    • Encourage preventive measures
    • Support public health initiatives
  • 4. What are the limitations of the Bamako initiative? (5 marks)
    • Lack of adequate funding
    • Reliance on user fees
    • Limited coverage in remote areas
    • Challenges in supply chain management
    • Inconsistent drug availability
  • 5. Differentiate between a formulary manual and treatment guidelines. (5 marks)
    • Formulary manual: Lists approved medicines for use
    • Treatment guidelines: Provide protocols for managing specific diseases or conditions
  • 6. List functions of the pharmacy and therapeutic committee. (5 marks)
    • Evaluate drug formulary requests
    • Monitor drug usage
    • Establish treatment guidelines
    • Promote rational drug use
    • Review adverse drug reactions
  • 7. Describe the basic issues to be considered for efficient, effective inventory management. (5 marks)
    • Accurate record-keeping
    • Regular stock assessments
    • Proper storage conditions
    • Timely reordering of stock
    • Minimizing waste and loss
  • 8. What are the benefits of inventory control? (5 marks)
    • Minimizes stockouts
    • Reduces wastage
    • Improves financial management
    • Ensures timely availability of drugs
    • Enhances efficiency in pharmacy operations
  • 9. Differentiate between independent and dependent demand systems. (3 marks)
    • Independent demand: Not reliant on other items
    • Dependent demand: Based on the need for other items
  • 10. What are the disadvantages of holding high stock levels? (2 marks)
    • Increased storage costs
    • Risk of obsolescence
    • Potential for wastage
  • 11. What are the advantages of using INN/Generic names? (5 marks)
    • Promotes drug accessibility
    • Cost-effective
    • Universally recognized
    • Enhances patient safety
    • Facilitates prescription practices
  • 12. (a) Discuss drug management cycle as truly a cycle. (10 marks)
    • Selection of essential medicines
    • Procurement of medicines
    • Distribution to healthcare facilities
    • Usage by healthcare providers and patients
    • Feedback for future selection and adjustments
  • (b) What are the goals of National Drug Policy? (5 marks)
    • Ensure drug availability
    • Promote drug affordability
    • Maintain drug quality
    • Encourage rational use of medicines
    • Strengthen healthcare systems
  • 13. (a) Discuss the steps in formulation of a National Drug Policy. (10 marks)
    • Assessing current drug needs
    • Setting policy objectives
    • Consulting stakeholders
    • Developing an implementation plan
    • Establishing monitoring and evaluation mechanisms
  • (b) List components or parts of a National Drug Policy. (5 marks)
    • Drug regulation
    • Supply chain management
    • Financing and affordability
    • Quality assurance
    • Promotion of rational drug use
  • 14. (a) What are the reasons for support of a limited essential drug list? (5 marks)
    • Cost-effectiveness
    • Simplified inventory management
    • Focus on essential medications
    • Better resource allocation
    • Improved access to critical medicines
  • (b) Describe the process of developing a formulary manual. (10 marks)
    • Evaluating drug efficacy
    • Consulting with stakeholders
    • Drafting guidelines
    • Ensuring compliance with regulations
    • Conducting regular reviews and updates
