Questions and Answers

  • 1. Discuss the elements of primary healthcare. (5mks)
    Answer: The elements include education on health issues, proper nutrition, safe water and sanitation, maternal and child care, immunization, disease control, and provision of essential drugs.
  • 2. List the principles of primary healthcare. (5mks)
    Answer: The principles are accessibility, community participation, health promotion, intersectoral collaboration, and appropriate technology.
  • 3. What is the role of community-based distribution programs in primary healthcare? (5mks)
    Answer: These programs increase access to healthcare, distribute medicines, provide health education, involve communities, and reduce central healthcare burdens.
  • 4. What are the limitations of the Bamako Initiative? (5mks)
    Answer: Limitations include reliance on external funding, potential financial barriers, limited sustainability, and inadequate community training.
  • 5. Differentiate between a formulary manual and treatment guidelines. (5mks)
    Answer: A formulary manual lists approved drugs, while treatment guidelines provide protocols for patient care.
  • 6. List functions of the pharmacy and therapeutic committee. (5mks)
    Answer: Functions include policy formulation, drug evaluation, managing formularies, and monitoring drug use and safety.
  • 7. Describe the basic issues to be considered for efficient, effective inventory management. (5mks)
    Answer: Key issues include demand forecasting, stock control, order scheduling, supplier management, and minimizing waste.
  • 8. What are the benefits of inventory control? (5mks)
    Answer: Benefits include reduced costs, optimal stock levels, minimized waste, improved service quality, and better financial planning.
  • 9. Differentiate between independent and dependent demand system. (3mks)
    Answer: Independent demand is unrelated to other items, while dependent demand is directly linked to other items' requirements.
  • 10. What are the disadvantages of holding high stock levels? (2mks)
    Answer: Disadvantages include high holding costs, risk of obsolescence, and reduced cash flow.
  • 11. What are the advantages of using INN/Generic name? (5mks)
    Answer: Advantages include cost-effectiveness, consistency, easier communication, reduced confusion, and broader accessibility.
  • 12a. Discuss drug management cycle as truly a cycle. (10mks)
    Answer: The drug management cycle involves continuous steps like selection, procurement, distribution, and use, feeding back into future improvements.
  • 12b. What are the goals of National Drug Policy? (5mks)
    Answer: Goals include ensuring drug quality, accessibility, affordability, rational use, and promoting local drug manufacturing.
  • 13a. Discuss the steps in formulation of a National Drug Policy. (10mks)
    Answer: Steps include situation analysis, policy drafting, stakeholder consultation, implementation planning, and monitoring.
  • 13b. List components or parts of a National Drug Policy. (5mks)
    Answer: Components include drug selection, procurement, distribution, regulation, and rational use.
  • 14a. What are the reasons for support of a limited essential drug list? (5mks)
    Answer: Reasons include cost control, focused healthcare, easier procurement, minimized waste, and improved stock management.
  • 14b. Describe the process of developing a formulary manual. (10mks)
    Answer: The process includes selecting drugs, establishing criteria, gathering expert input, testing guidelines, and regular review.
